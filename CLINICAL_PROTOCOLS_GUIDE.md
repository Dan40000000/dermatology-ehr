# Clinical Protocols Quick Reference Guide

## Medical Dermatology Protocols

### 1. Acne Treatment Ladder
**Use for:** All acne severities
**Steps:**
1. Initial assessment
2. Mild: Tretinoin 0.025% gel nightly
3. Add: Benzoyl peroxide 5% gel morning
4. Moderate: Doxycycline 100mg BID for 12-16 weeks
5. Severe: Consider isotretinoin referral
6. Pre-isotretinoin workup (CBC, CMP, lipids, pregnancy test)
7. Isotretinoin 0.5-1 mg/kg/day for 20 weeks

**Key Points:**
- Avoid isotretinoin in pregnancy (Category X)
- Limit doxycycline to 3-4 months
- iPLEDGE required for isotretinoin

---

### 2. Psoriasis Treatment Algorithm
**Use for:** Plaque psoriasis
**Steps:**
1. Assess BSA and PASI score
2. Mild (<3% BSA): Triamcinolone 0.1% BID + Calcipotriene
3. Moderate (3-10% BSA): Add phototherapy (NB-UVB 3x/week)
4. Severe (>10% BSA): Pre-biologic workup
5. Start TNF inhibitor (Adalimumab 40mg Q2 weeks)
6. If inadequate response: IL-17 or IL-23 inhibitor

**Pre-Biologic Screening:**
- TB test (Quantiferon-TB Gold)
- Hepatitis B & C panel
- CBC, CMP
- HIV (optional but recommended)

---

### 3. Atopic Dermatitis Management
**Use for:** Eczema/atopic dermatitis
**Steps:**
1. Baseline EASI or SCORAD assessment
2. Foundation: Thick moisturizers (Vanicream, CeraVe) BID
3. Mild-moderate: Triamcinolone 0.1% (body) or Hydrocortisone 2.5% (face)
4. Maintenance: Tacrolimus 0.1% ointment BID
5. Widespread: NB-UVB phototherapy 2-3x/week
6. Severe: Dupilumab 600mg loading, then 300mg Q2 weeks

**Key Points:**
- Moisturizers are ALWAYS the foundation
- Low-potency steroids on face
- Dupilumab for moderate-severe AD

---

### 4. Melanoma Surveillance Schedule
**Use for:** Post-treatment melanoma patients

| Stage | Frequency | Imaging |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Stage 0 (in situ) | Annually | None |
| Stage I-II | Q6 months x 5 years, then annually | Usually none |
| Stage III-IV | Q3-4 months x 2 years, then Q6 months | PET/CT or CT Q6-12 months |

---

### 5. Pre-Biologic Workup Protocol
**Required before:** Any biologic therapy
**Labs:**
- Quantiferon-TB Gold + Chest X-ray
- Hepatitis B surface antigen, core antibody
- Hepatitis C antibody
- CBC with differential
- CMP
- Pregnancy test (females of childbearing age)

**Vaccinations:**
- Complete BEFORE starting biologic
- NO live vaccines after starting
- Update: Flu, pneumonia, shingles

---

### 6. Isotretinoin (Accutane) Protocol
**Use for:** Severe nodular acne, treatment-resistant acne
**Absolute Contraindications:** Pregnancy, severe depression

**iPLEDGE Requirements:**
1. Enrollment (patient, prescriber, pharmacy)
2. Two negative pregnancy tests before starting
3. Two forms of contraception (1 month before, during, and 1 month after)
4. Monthly pregnancy tests
5. Monthly attestation in iPLEDGE system

**Labs:**
- Baseline: CBC, CMP, lipids, pregnancy test
- Monthly: Pregnancy test (females)
- Q4-8 weeks: CBC, CMP, lipids

**Dosing:** 0.5-1 mg/kg/day (typically 40-80mg) for 5-6 months
**Target:** Cumulative dose 120-150 mg/kg

**Side Effects:**
- Dry skin, lips, eyes (universal)
- Elevated triglycerides (common)
- Elevated LFTs (monitor)
- Mood changes (monitor closely)

---

### 7. Methotrexate Monitoring Protocol
**Use for:** Psoriasis, severe eczema
**Contraindications:** Pregnancy, liver disease, immunodeficiency

**Dosing:** Start 7.5-15mg weekly + Folic acid 1mg daily

**Monitoring Schedule:**
- Baseline: CBC, CMP, hepatitis panel, pregnancy test
- Weeks 1-4: CBC, CMP weekly
- Months 2-3: CBC, CMP monthly
- Stable: CBC, CMP every 3 months

**Key Points:**
- AVOID ALCOHOL
- Take folic acid daily
- Watch for low WBC/platelets, elevated AST/ALT

## Procedure Protocols

### 8. Skin Biopsy Protocol
**Indications:** Suspicious or diagnostic lesions

**Technique Selection:**
- **Punch:** Small lesions, inflammatory conditions
- **Shave:** Raised, benign-appearing lesions
- **Excisional:** Large lesions, melanoma concern

**Steps:**
1. Informed consent
2. Prep with alcohol or chlorhexidine
3. Anesthetize: 1% lidocaine with epinephrine (avoid on digits, ears, nose)
4. Perform biopsy
5. Hemostasis: Aluminum chloride, cautery, or sutures
6. Place in formalin, label correctly
7. Post-procedure: Petrolatum + bandage, suture removal 7-14 days

---

### 9. Cryotherapy Protocol (Liquid Nitrogen)

| Lesion Type | Freeze Time | Technique |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| Actinic Keratosis | 5-10 sec | 1mm halo, may repeat |
| Seborrheic Keratosis | 10-15 sec | Allow full thaw, repeat |
| Common Wart | 20-30 sec | 2mm halo, may need repeat |
| Plantar Wart | 30+ sec | Aggressive, expect blister |

**Post-Cryo:** Blister is normal, takes 2-4 weeks to resolve

---

### 10. Intralesional Injection Protocol (Triamcinolone)

| Indication | Concentration | Volume | Frequency |
|------------|---------------|---------|-----------|
| Keloid/Hypertrophic Scar | 10-40 mg/mL | Inject until blanches | Q4-6 weeks |
| Alopecia Areata | 5 mg/mL | 0.1mL per injection, 1cm apart | Q4-6 weeks |
| Acne Cyst | 2.5-5 mg/mL | 0.1-0.2mL per cyst | One time |

**Side Effects:** Atrophy (usually temporary), hypopigmentation

## Cosmetic Protocols

### 11. Botox Upper Face - Standard Units

| Area | Units | Injection Points | Key Safety |
|------|-------|------------------|------------|
| Glabella (frown) | 20-25 | 5 points (2 corrugator, 3 procerus) | Stay >1cm above orbital rim |
| Forehead | 10-20 | 4-8 points, 2cm above brow | Keep lateral high to avoid ptosis |
| Crow's Feet | 12-15 per side | 3 points, 1cm from orbital rim | Stay lateral to avoid diplopia |

**Onset:** 3-5 days
**Peak:** 2 weeks
**Duration:** 3-4 months

**Post-Injection:** No lying down x 4 hours, no exercise x 24 hours

---

### 12. Dermal Filler - Nasolabial Folds
**Product:** Juvederm Ultra Plus or Restylane
**Volume:** 0.5-1.0 mL per side
**Technique:** Linear threading or serial puncture, deep dermal/supraperiosteal

**DANGER ZONE:** Facial artery runs along nasolabial fold
- Always aspirate before injecting
- Be alert for vascular occlusion

**VASCULAR OCCLUSION EMERGENCY:**
- Signs: Severe pain, blanching, mottling, vision changes
- Action: STOP immediately, inject hyaluronidase, call for help

---

### 13. Chemical Peel Selection

| Peel Type | Indications | Downtime | Skin Types |
|-----------|-------------|----------|------------|
| Glycolic 30-70% | Photoaging, fine lines | Minimal | All types |
| Salicylic 20-30% | Acne, oily skin | Minimal | All types, safe for darker |
| TCA 20-35% | Moderate photoaging, AKs | 3-7 days | I-IV (risk in V-VI) |

**Pre-Peel:** Valacyclovir 500mg BID x 2 days before, continue 7 days (HSV prophylaxis)

### 14. Laser Hair Removal by Skin Type

| Fitzpatrick | Laser | Starting Fluence | Notes |
|-------------|-------|------------------|-------|
| I-II (very light) | Alexandrite 755nm | 15-20 J/cm² | Low PIH risk |
| III-IV (olive-light brown) | Diode 810nm or Nd:YAG 1064nm | 12-15 J/cm² | Higher PIH risk, test spot |
| V-VI (dark brown-black) | Nd:YAG 1064nm ONLY | 10-12 J/cm² | HIGH PIH RISK, pre-treat with hydroquinone |

**Key:** Test spot and wait 2 weeks for dark skin

## Administrative Protocols

### 15. Post-Mohs Wound Care
**Timeline:**
- **Day 0-1:** Keep pressure dressing dry, intact. Elevate if on extremity.
- **Day 1+:** Gently clean with soap/water BID. Apply petrolatum. Cover with bandage.
- **Watch for:** Increasing pain, spreading redness, pus, fever, uncontrolled bleeding

**Suture Removal:**
- Face: 7 days
- Body: 10-14 days
- Legs: 14 days

## Quick Reference: Common Dosing

### Oral Antibiotics (Acne)
- Doxycycline: 100mg BID (limit to 12-16 weeks)
- Minocycline: 100mg BID
- Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole: DS BID

### Topical Retinoids
- Tretinoin: 0.025% → 0.05% → 0.1% gel
- Adapalene: 0.1% → 0.3% gel
- Tazarotene: 0.05% → 0.1% cream

### Topical Steroids by Potency
- **Ultra-high:** Clobetasol 0.05%
- **High:** Triamcinolone 0.5%
- **Mid:** Triamcinolone 0.1%
- **Low:** Hydrocortisone 2.5%
- **Ultra-low:** Hydrocortisone 1%

### Biologics - Common Dosing
- Adalimumab (Humira): 80mg → 40mg Q2 weeks
- Dupilumab (Dupixent): 600mg → 300mg Q2 weeks
- Secukinumab (Cosentyx): 300mg weekly x 5, then monthly
- Guselkumab (Tremfya): 100mg Q8 weeks

## Safety Alerts

### Never/Always Rules
- ❌ **NEVER** isotretinoin in pregnancy (Category X)
- ❌ **NEVER** epinephrine on digits, ears, nose, penis
- ❌ **NEVER** high-potency steroids on face for >2 weeks
- ❌ **NEVER** skip iPLEDGE for isotretinoin
- ✅ **ALWAYS** aspirate before dermal filler injection
- ✅ **ALWAYS** screen for TB before biologics
- ✅ **ALWAYS** use two forms of contraception with isotretinoin
- ✅ **ALWAYS** have hyaluronidase available when injecting filler

### High-Risk Medications
1. **Isotretinoin:** iPLEDGE, pregnancy prevention, lipid monitoring
2. **Methotrexate:** Avoid alcohol, folic acid, frequent labs
3. **Biologics:** TB screening, infection risk, no live vaccines
